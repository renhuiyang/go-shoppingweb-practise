How Laborelief Works for Pain Relief During Labor

How can something so small help in such a big way? And what does a mouth-piece device have to do with giving birth? These are the two most common questions about how Laborelief works.
Laborelief provides effective labor pain management by working from three vantage points:
1. Oxygen increase
2. Effective Pushing
3. Easier Dilation
Below we delineate these three points.

Oxygen Increase - Easier Dilation, Better Pushing
Heard of all those breathing techniques for birth? The problem is that breathing techniques can take a lot of practice in order to provide solid pain relief during labor. One reason to take advantage of Laborelief is that the device easily keeps your mouth slightly open, leading to more optimal breathing and hence, more oxygen. (You won't need to practice any techniques because you'll just automatically breathe better with Laborelief in position.)
Increased oxygen levels translate into more effective muscle function, whether during the dilation stage or the pushing stage. And better yet, it's your own, naturally breathed-in, oxygen – no wall unit pump necessary.

Effective Pushing by Biting Down
There's nothing like pushing a baby out to allow you to actualize powers you never even knew you had. Whether you feel the instinct to push, or are directed by medical staff, you'll likely be bearing down in order to bring your baby out to be born. One way to bear down effectively is to bite down on something, and the Laborelief devices are specifically and comfortably designed for this. Consequently, the bearing-down biting action facilitates your abdominal and pelvic area muscles to bear down better for pushing. In addition, the opportunity to bite down intensely on something comfortably placed in your mouth provides a focused, physical channel for your pushing efforts.

Easier Dilation: Physical Relaxation and the "O" Mouth Shape
According to Ina May Gaskin, the most well-known midwife in the United States (if not the world) writes about the Sphincter Law: Opening the mouth like an "O" is directly equated with opening the cervix wider. The Laborelief device keeps the mouth open in the "O" formation, setting the stage for quicker dilation.
The other way to promote easier dilation - as well as pain relief during labor - is to physically relax the body. Relaxation enables the two primary labor hormones, oxytocin and relaxin, to flow best. (In contrast, adrenaline, the "stress hormone" impedes oxytocin and relaxin.) Relaxation also allows endorphins to be released – allowing the woman to even feel pleasure.
